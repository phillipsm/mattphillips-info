an investigation of digital stereoscopic photography

About

We see in stereo. We have two eyes and each one captures its own image as we navigate the world. Our brains wire the two images together providing visuals rich with depth, texture, and increased dimensionality.

What if our cameras could see the way we see? They can. And, it's not difficult - we simply need to add lenses.

This is an investigation in how to build an open, affordable, digital stereoscopic camera.

Technology

I have created two prototypes that leverage the Arduino platform. Both prototypes use four cameras, wired to an SD module, controlled through a microcontroller.

The first prototype is built around an Arduino Mega and is housed in a plastic project box. This prototype functions, but is rather delicate due to its exposed wires and battery.

The second prototype uses Adafruit's Metro Mini and is fitted between three layers of quarter inch, laser cut acrylic. I also added a rechargable battery. This iteration is more robust, better looking, and much more usable in the field.

Source

Source code is available through a dual MIT/GPL license on GitHub.

I've also bundled the templates I used to laser cut the acrylic plates in the latest prototype. Enjoy.
